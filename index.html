From Shade to Stone. Every Step Matters.

Image: Tea plants under shade canopies

Shade-Grown

Three weeks before harvest, the tea plants are covered with traditional canopies. Reduced sunlight triggers the leaves to produce more L-theanine and chlorophyll — the compounds responsible for matcha's calm energy and vivid green color.

First Harvest Only

We use only ichiban-cha — the season's very first picking, when the leaves are youngest, most tender, and richest in flavor. Many brands blend first and second harvest. We never do.

Image: Young tea leaves at first picking
Image: Traditional granite stone mill

Stone-Milled

Our matcha is ground on traditional granite mills that turn slowly — about 40 grams per hour — to avoid heat damage. Heat degrades color, flavor, and nutrients. Speed is the enemy of quality. We choose patience.

Tested and Certified

USDA Organic Certified. Lab-tested for heavy metals and microbial safety. California Prop 65 compliant. We publish what others won't because we have nothing to hide.
